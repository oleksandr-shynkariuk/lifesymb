Motion


Focus: Flexibility and coordination
Metoder: Movement variations; Wing Tsun basics; ReAction training; Complex movements

Short description: The motion course increases the variety of possible movements of the participants. We will also work with coordinative tasks, which train the ability to concentrate. The ReAction training, different movement styles and elements from dancing, martial arts, gymnastics and ball games are used during this course.
A key feature of our independence as human beings is mobility. We define mobility as the ability to independently and safely move oneself from one place to another. Mobility incorporates many types of tasks, including the ability to stand up from a bed or chair, to walk or run, and to navigate through often quite complex environments.

Gait is an extraordinarily complex behavior. It involves the entire body and therefore requires the coordination of many muscles and joints. Navigating through complex and often cluttered environments requires the use of sensory inputs to assist in the control and adaptation of gait. Finally, movement behavior includes the ability to initiate and terminate movement, to adapt gait to avoid obstacles, and to change speed and direction as needed.

On the foundation of a proper stance and gait, the participants will learn new movements to increase their variety of movement choices. Unexpected situations always require a wide array of possible solutions of which one can choose.

Under these considerations it is clear, that more options will lead to better and more effective movement behaviours. Besides, being able to move freely inside the surrounding environment seems to be obvious. However, if you loose the ability to do so, you will definitely miss it.
